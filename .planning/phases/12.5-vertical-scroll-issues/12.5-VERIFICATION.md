**Test:** Open the app (`npm run dev` in `Application/`), load any project. Inspect the timeline panel right edge.

#### 2. Thumb Position Reactivity

**Test:** Scroll the timeline vertically with the mouse wheel. Observe the scrollbar thumb.
**Expected:** Thumb moves proportionally — thumb at top when scrolled to top, thumb at bottom when scrolled to bottom of content.
**Why human:** Reactive signal-to-DOM rendering must be observed live; computed geometry depends on actual rendered sizes.

#### 3. Thumb Drag Behavior

**Test:** Click and drag the scrollbar thumb. Move cursor outside the thumb element while dragging.
**Expected:** Timeline scrolls while dragging. Drag continues to work even when cursor leaves the thumb (pointer capture active).
**Why human:** Pointer capture behavior is a browser API that must be tested interactively.

#### 4. Track Click Jump

**Test:** Scroll partway down. Click an empty area of the scrollbar (not the thumb).
**Expected:** ScrollY jumps instantly to the proportional position matching the click location.
**Why human:** Requires distinguishing click target (thumb vs track) at runtime; depends on rendered element bounds.

#### 5. Auto-Scroll During Playback

**Test:** Load a project with 3+ content sequences. Scroll so that later sequences are off-screen. Press play. Let playback advance into the hidden sequence.
**Expected:** Timeline snaps instantly (no smooth animation/transition) to show the active sequence track. Canvas renders correctly throughout.
**Why human:** Requires sufficient track count to trigger overflow, real-time playback, and timing-dependent cross-boundary detection.
