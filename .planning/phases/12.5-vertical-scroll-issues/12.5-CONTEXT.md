# Phase 12.5: Vertical Scroll Issues - Context

**Gathered:** 2026-03-18
**Status:** Ready for planning

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Fix the timeline canvas vertical scrolling — with 3+ content sequences (or enough FX tracks), bottom tracks get clipped off-screen with no way to scroll or visual indication of overflow. Add a custom vertical scrollbar and auto-scroll during playback.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Scrollbar rendering
- Pure DOM element next to the canvas — NOT drawn on canvas, NOT an HTML overlay on top of the canvas
- Add the scrollbar div as a sibling to the TimelineCanvas inside the canvas container, using flex-row layout
- 4px wide, positioned at the right edge of the timeline panel
- Spans the full timeline height (including ruler area), not just the track area — future-proofing for resizable timeline panel

### Scrollbar appearance
- Always visible, even when all tracks fit on screen (no conditional hide)
- Thumb color: reuse `--sidebar-scrollbar-thumb` CSS variable for app-wide consistency
- Thumb border-radius: 2px (match sidebar pattern)

### Scrollbar interaction
- Thumb is draggable — click and drag to scroll vertically
- Clicking the scrollbar track (not the thumb) jumps scrollY to that relative position
- Both drag and click-to-jump interactions work together

### Auto vertical scroll during playback
- During playback, when the playhead enters a sequence whose track is off-screen, auto-scroll vertically to show that track
- Snap instantly (no animation) — matches how horizontal auto-scroll (`ensureFrameVisible`) works
- Vertical equivalent of `ensureFrameVisible` for scrollY: `ensureTrackVisible(sequenceId)`

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact thumb minimum height calculation
- Scroll momentum/smoothing on wheel events (existing wheel handler already works)
- Track header behavior during vertical scroll (currently headers scroll with content via ctx.translate — keep as-is)

</decisions>

## Specific Ideas

- The scrollbar should be a simple standalone Preact component (e.g., `TimelineScrollbar.tsx`) that reads/writes `timelineStore.scrollY`

- Timeline panel may become resizable in the future (grab icon to expand/collapse) — scrollbar spanning full height anticipates this
