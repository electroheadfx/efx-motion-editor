# Phase 36.12 Plan 11: Final Generated Roto Interpolation Closure Summary

Phase 36.12 is closed after automated gates and user-approved live UAT. The final UAT blocker was the source/display contract for new real keys created while interpolation was enabled: distant display-frame edits could appear stale, generated-like, or stranded at absolute display positions after disabling interpolation. The accepted product decision is that Roto real keys form a compact source-key sequence.

## What Changed

- `getSourceRotoFrameForDisplayFrame()` now maps empty display-frame saves while interpolation is enabled to the next compact source slot.
- `PhysicsPaintStudio` now normalizes saved Roto frames to source coordinates, refreshes cached Roto frames from the regenerated store mapping, updates occupied/saved markers, updates editable-frame state, and moves the current frame to the regenerated display position for the saved real key.
- Store regression coverage now proves a distant save while interpolation is enabled appends into compact source order, remains a real key, collapses to compact order when interpolation is disabled, and survives save/load.
- Studio regression coverage now requires regenerated-cache live refresh after `Save current`.

## Deviations from Plan

### User-driven compact sequence correction

- **Observed during:** Final live UAT after earlier toggle-off and persistence fixes.
- **Issue:** A new real key created at a distant display frame while interpolation was enabled could fail to appear as a live green key, could look generated-like until reopen, or could remain stranded at its absolute display position when interpolation was disabled.
- **Decision:** Roto real keys are compact source-sequence keys. Distant display-frame edits append into the compact source sequence.
- **Fix:** Changed display-to-source resolution for enabled interpolation and refreshed Studio live cache/markers/current-frame from regenerated store frames after save.
- **Verification:** Focused matrix, typecheck, diff check, and user approval passed.
