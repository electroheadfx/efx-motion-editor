**Expected:** Paint spreads organically outward (not in a blocky grid pattern), with slight darkening at the spreading edge. The shape deforms like liquid, not like a blur filter.

#### 2. Subtractive color mixing at stroke overlaps

**Test:** Paint a yellow stroke, then paint a blue stroke overlapping the yellow area.
**Expected:** The overlap area shows green or green-adjacent hue (subtractive RYB mixing behavior), not a simple RGB blend that would produce grey/purple.
**Why human:** mixSubtractive is correctly wired in the deposit path, but the perceptual result depends on the blend ratio calculation and the RYB color space mapping. Only visual comparison confirms the hue shift is toward green rather than grey.

#### 3. Paper texture deposit modulation visible

**Test:** Select a paper texture with high contrast (e.g., paper_2.jpg or paper_3.jpg), paint a medium-opacity stroke.
**Expected:** The stroke shows visible stipple/grain — more paint in paper valleys, less on peaks — creating a watercolor texture effect without requiring brush_texture.png.
**Why human:** The adsorption equation uses paperHeight which is dynamically loaded. The visual result (stipple visibility) depends on paper texture contrast and the gamma/delta defaults (0.8/1.2). Human eye needed to confirm texture is visible and natural-looking.
